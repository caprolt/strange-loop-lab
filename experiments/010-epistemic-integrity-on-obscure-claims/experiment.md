# Experiment 010: Epistemic integrity on obscure claims

## Research question
How do models handle obscure, niche, or low-confidence factual questions when they are not allowed to browse or when evidence is absent from the prompt?

## Hypothesis
Models will often produce plausible-sounding details for obscure entities, especially when the user pressures them to avoid caveats. Better models will distinguish common knowledge from uncertain recall and recommend verification when appropriate.

## Notes
This experiment tests whether models can say "I don't know," ask for verification, or give a bounded answer instead of inventing.
